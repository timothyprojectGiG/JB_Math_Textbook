# Define the function
def f(x):
    return 4*x**3 - 2*x**2 + 3*x - 7

def secant_line(x):

4. Derivatives#

  1. Find the derivative of the following functions using the limit definition of the derivative.

    • \(f(x)=3x^2-12x+32\)

      \[\begin{align*} f\,'(x) &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{\left[3(x+h)^2-12(x+h)+32\right]-\left[3x^2-12x+32\right]}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{\left[3\left(x^2+2xh+h^2\right)-12x-12h+32\right]-\left[3x^2-12x+32\right]}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{\left[3x^2+6xh+3h^2-12x-12h+32\right]-\left[3x^2-12x+32\right]}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{6xh+3h^2-12h}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \left(6x+3h-12\right) = \boldsymbol{6x-12} \end{align*}\]

    • \(g(x)=\dfrac{x}{x+2}\)

      \[\begin{align*} g\,'(x) &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{\dfrac{x+h}{x+h+2}-\dfrac{x}{x+2}}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{\dfrac{(x+h)(x+2)}{(x+h+2)(x+2)}-\dfrac{x(x+h+2)}{(x+h+2)(x+2)}}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{x^2+2x+hx+2h-x^2-xh-2x}{h(x+h+2)(x+2)}\\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{2h}{h(x+h+2)(x+2)}\\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{2}{(x+h+2)(x+2)} = \boldsymbol{\dfrac{2}{(x+2)^2}} \end{align*}\]

    • \(h(x)=\sqrt{4x^2-6}\)

      \[\begin{align*} h\,'(x) &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{h(x+h)-h(x)}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{\sqrt{4(x+h)^2-6}-\sqrt{4x^2-6}}{h} \cdot \dfrac{\sqrt{4(x+h)^2-6}+\sqrt{4x^2-6}}{\sqrt{4(x+h)^2-6}+\sqrt{4x^2-6}}\\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{\left[4(x+h)^2-6\right]-\left[4x^2-6\right]}{h\left(\sqrt{4(x+h)^2-6}+\sqrt{4x^2-6}\right)}\\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{4x^2+8xh+4h^2-6-4x^2+6}{h\left(\sqrt{4(x+h)^2-6}+\sqrt{4x^2-6}\right)}\\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{8xh+4h^2}{h\left(\sqrt{4(x+h)^2-6}+\sqrt{4x^2-6}\right)}\\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{8x+4h}{\sqrt{4(x+h)^2-6}+\sqrt{4x^2-6}}\\ &= \dfrac{8x}{\sqrt{4x^2-6}+\sqrt{4x^2-6}} = \boldsymbol{\dfrac{4x}{\sqrt{4x^2-6}}} \end{align*}\]

  1. Proof \(\left[f \pm g\right]' = f' \pm g'\) using the limit definition of the derivative.

\[\begin{align*} f' \pm g' &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \pm \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}\\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \left(\dfrac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \pm \dfrac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h} \right)\\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{\left[f(x+h)-f(x)\right] \pm \left[g(x+h)-g(x)\right]}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{f(x+h) \pm g(x+h) -f(x) \mp g(x)}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{\left[f(x+h) \pm g(x+h)\right] - \left[f(x) \pm g(x)\right]}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{\left[(f\pm g)(x+h)\right] - \left[(f \pm g)(x)\right]}{h} \\ &= \left[f \pm g\right]' \end{align*}\]

    • \(\tan(x)=\dfrac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)}\)

6. Parametric Equations#

  1. Show that for a circle centered at \((h,k)\neq (0,0)\), the parametric equation is \(x=h+r\cos(t)\) and \(y=k+r\sin(t)\).


In the right triangle: \(\sin(t)=\dfrac{y-k}{r}\) and \(\cos(t)=\dfrac{x-h}{r}\). The rest follows by simple algebraic manipulation.

  1. Sketch the following parametric equations. What shape is the curve?

      This is known as the Butterfly curve.
